DONATIONS

BLOOD DONATION

Blood donation refers to the process of collecting, testing, preparing and storing blood and blood components.

KIDNEY DONATION

A donated kidney is removed from a donor and surgically implanted in a person with kidney failure.

LIVER DONATION

A liver donation is surgery to remove your disease or injured liver and replace it with a healthy liver from another person.

PANCREAS DONATION

A healthy pancreas from a deceased donor can be transplanted to another person.

OTHER ORGANS DONATON

Donation of other organs like lungs, liver, kidney , pancreas and small bowel.

FULL BODY DONATION

The donation of the whole body after death for purposes of medical research and education

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is organ donation?

It is the process in which a person is provided with the gift to give organs to save other lives. The donor can be either alive or dead.

TYPES

1.Living Donor Organ Donation
2. Deceased Organ Donation
3. Tissue Donation

HOW CAN I BE A DONOR?

You can be a donor by registering with any authorized organ donation form. Prior arrangements should be made for the same.

EFFECT ON APPEARANCE

Donation does not disfigure the body or interfere with open casket funeral arrangements.

BARRIERS TO DONATION

In most cases you can be a donor. Existing medical condition does not necessarily prevent a person from becoming an organ or tissue donor.

DONOR's SCREENING

Blood is taken from all potential donor and tested to rule out transmissible diseases and viruses such as HIV and hepatitis.
